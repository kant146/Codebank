Viral Digital Ads Of 2021 – Internet Chaos Of Everyday

Advertisements have a great impact on our minds and ultimately affect our buying behaviour. We love them, criticize them, and share them a lot in the process. Up next are a few such ads that went viral in 2021 and we are living for it!

Signal-ling A Paradigmatic Shift

Just when Facebook HQ was worried about the privacy concerns raised against them, Signal launched their thoughtfully crafted social campaigns solely directed towards the importance of “Privacy”. Endorsements sprinkled by Elon Musk and Edward Snowden, of course, the shift took the world by storm. And people could see why it was the best option out of the two, hence a wave of more than 7 million downloads was observed in a week’s duration since WhatsApp’s leaks in their privacy policy. Telegram, another end-to-end encrypted cloud messaging app received millions of downloads in the wail of keeping the world’s data private.

Have You Met, CRED Yet?

CRED has always provoked the mass with its not-so-subtle advertising! This year, we all witnessed how CRED did it with their humour. The casting of renowned faces at their unusual played the trump card. Be it the Indian cricketer Rahul Dravid at his most aggressive behaviour stuck in traffic throwing his “Bats in the air” (pun intended), or the unthinkable casting of the Olympics gold medalist Neeraj Chopra – stunned the most of us!

Driving Around With Timothee Scissorhands

An ode to the Tim Buton’s classic tale of “Edward Scissorhands” – the epoch Cadillac’s ad on its brand new Lyriq series this SuperBowl season took many of us down the memory lane. The commercial could be seen as a sequel of the film as Winona Ryder reprises her role, playing Kim Boggs again. Starring Timothee Chalamet as her and Edward’s son – as Edgar Scissorhands. Come on, we weren’t the only ones to shed a tear when Kim showed Edgard the only thing where his peculiar hands won’t come in the way.

Fumble, Said Apple

As always, Apple stands out with a commercial for its iPhone 12. To promote the durability of the iPhone 12 and its Ceramic Shield, Apple plays an unforgettable Indian classical beat — ‘The Conference’ by musician Nitin Sawhney. In the ad, we see a lady fumble and try her best not to let the iPhone fall onto the pavement but fails. Cut to a scene where irrespective of the terrible fall the phone’s screen is intact and she is relieved to see the phone in one piece. The sequence stays imprinted on your memory long after the 38-second ad is over!

Hon’ble mentions

The Shower directed by Bauddhayan Mukherji & The Letter directed by Aarsha Mukherji

This year at Moscow’s Lampa Film Festival, an unusual coincidence took place as two Mukherji’s from the same family won awards in two different categories. Father Bauddhayan Mukherji and daughter Aarsha Mukherji for their ad films “The Shower” and “The Letter” won awards respectively. Where Mr Mukherji’s The Shower shows the negligence behind water waste, her daughter projects the humans’ ruthless behaviour to Mother Earth that causes us horrible natural calamities.

Bauddhayan’s “The Shower” is the first Indian ad film to get a National Award. “The Letter” was chosen by UNICEF to release on Children’s Day across Maharashtra last year. It was also awarded for films made by children at the Psaroloco Film Festival in Greece in August.

Aaand CUT!

2021 has stood out as another step up in the advertising industry. Some bagged awards, some gathered controversy, whereas some films ended up being meme templates and were conversation starters! Creativity prospers in different ways. Especially, when we are living in the digital era only can we wholeheartedly agree to that.
